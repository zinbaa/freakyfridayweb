ATTRACTIONS AND ACTIVITIES

LUNA PARK

Luna Park is Sydney's premier amusement park featuring fun and games for the entire family. Located at Milsons Point on the north side of Sydney Harbour bridge the park has an amazing outlook over Sydney Harbour. Entry to Luna Park is free so you only need to pay for rides and games.

Some of Luna Park's buildings and attractions are so etched in Sydney's history that they are now heritage-protected by the National Trust. After some decades of adversity the park now continues in its role in delighting visitors to the harbour city.

Read more: https://www.lunaparksydney.com



TARONGA ZOO

One of the most famous attractions in Sydney, the award-winning Taronga Zoo is home to over 4,000 animals, including Australian native wildlife, as well as rare and endangered exotic animals. Taronga Zoo is just 12 minutes from the city by ferry overlooking Sydney's stunning harbor. Open 365 days a year, recording daily conversations and shows, as well as access to Sky Safari, Sydney's only cable car.

Enjoy the QBE Free Flight Bird Show overlooking Sydney Harbor, featuring some of the world's most spectacular birds and enjoying the Daily Seal Show where Australian and California sea lions and New Zealand Fur seals will impress you with their grace and skill.

Challenge yourself with Taronga's exciting new sky-high adventure, Wild Ropes. Overcome obstacles, cross spring bridges and soar through the trees while enjoying breathtaking views of Sydney Harbor.

Read more: http://www.taronga.org.au



SYDNEY'S OPERA HOUSE

The Sydney Opera House, located by the harbor, is regularly mentioned as one of the greatest architectural works of the 20th century, and is also listed on the World Heritage List. The Opera House has four major companies based there: Opera Australia, The Australian Ballet, Sydney Theater Company and the Sydney Symphony Orchestra. Over one million people come here to see performances every year, while many more millions come to admire the building, which is a symphony in itself.

The Opera House is surrounded by Sydney Harbor on three sides, and the view from the monument's stairs is truly dramatic. Take a break on the stairs and photograph the harbor and harbor bridge in Sydney. Also, make sure that you get some good pictures of the opera house's famous "braid", which the white shells behind you make up.

Much of the inside of the opera house is freely accessible, so just look around. In the reception area you can see the beautiful floor-to-ceiling tapestries, which are inspired by Bach's Hamburg symphonies.

Read more: https://www.sydneyoperahouse.com



WESTFIELD SYDNEY SHOPPING

Shopping and dining in Australia begins at Westfield Sydney, with over 250 fashion, food and lifestyle shops, located in central Sydney. Westfield Sydney offers a mix of top Australian designers and international high street fashion across four levels, with three more eateries, and is the ultimate shopping and dining venue.

To welcome visitors to Sydney, Westfield has created the Westfield Sydney Privilege Card, which offers visitors exclusive discounts in Sydney, special offers and access to their Concierge Team. The Westfield Sydney Privilege Card is available for out-of-town guests presenting their driver's license or passport at the street-level Concierge Desk on either side of the Pitt Street Mall.

Westfields vennlige concierge-ansatte vil gi deg en liste over spesialtilbud og en senterkatalog for din bekvemmelighet, så alt du trenger å gjøre er å begynne å handle!
